First 90 Days

Enterprise Service Lead · Learn the mission, align the architecture, release trusted capability

Operating intent

The first 90 days should reduce uncertainty before increasing solution volume. Begin with analyst decisions and service dependencies, install governance and architecture mechanisms, and then advance prototypes toward operational release.

Days 0-30

Immersion & discovery
  • Listen to CBP analysts, mission owners, enterprise architects, security, data, cloud and program leadership.
  • Map operational questions, current services, data flows, cross-domain constraints, decision rights, dependencies and migration-wave status.
  • Identify where work stalls: access, lineage, environment readiness, platform fit, explainability, integration, adoption or ownership.
  • Establish cadence for the Cross-Domain Working Group, backlog, risk review and executive briefing.
Outputs: Mission decision map · stakeholder map · current-state service topology · risk and dependency register.

Days 31-60

Align & architect
  • Prioritize capabilities using mission value, feasibility, data readiness, governance burden, reuse potential and time-to-value.
  • Define reference architecture patterns for natural-language analytics, graph/network analysis, streaming, model feedback, lineage and human review.
  • Align each capability to the Cloud Migration gate and define evidence needed to advance.
  • Build a prototype backlog with decision owners, exit criteria, security assumptions, adoption requirements and measurement categories.
Outputs: Capability roadmap · reference architectures · explainability and lineage baseline · evidence backlog.

Days 61-90

Deliver & operationalize
  • Advance highest-value prototypes through the Cross-Domain Service Airlock with complete mission, data, model, security, human-authority and ownership evidence.
  • Stand up analyst feedback and model-correction loops; define how feedback becomes training data, service change, policy review or a stop decision.
  • Validate enterprise integration, observability, recovery, documentation, training and transition ownership before operational release.
  • Produce executive capability assessments, readiness recommendations, deployment choices and the next-quarter investment view.
  • Coach teams on experimentation discipline, human-centered design, measurable value delivery and learning capture.
Outputs: Capability scorecard · analyst feedback loop · deployment recommendation · next-wave investment brief.

Decision cadence

WeeklyCapability backlog, blocked dependencies, risk and evidence review.
BiweeklyCross-Domain Working Group architecture, access, governance and integration decisions.
MonthlyExecutive capability assessment: mission value, readiness, risk, adoption, ownership and investment choice.
Per releaseAirlock review with explicit advance, hold, rework or stop decision and recorded rationale.

Balanced scorecard measurement categories

  • Mission outcome: decision quality, timeliness and analyst usefulness.
  • Trust: explainability, lineage completeness, human authority, auditability and policy compliance.
  • Flow: cycle time between idea, prototype, gate decision, integration and operational release.
  • Adoption: analyst use, correction behavior, training readiness, support burden and ownership clarity.
  • Learning: reusable architecture patterns, repeated failure modes, feedback captured and stop decisions made early.
Day-90 test: The team can explain which capabilities matter most, where each sits in the migration and release sequence, what evidence is missing, who owns the decision, what the analyst receives and what must be learned next.
