This is a documented technical-incompatibility fallback, not a claim that the typeset identifier is the official logo.

## Typography evidence and decision
GDIT's public site uses condensed, forceful uppercase display typography paired with a practical sans-serif body system. The campaign uses the open-source `Barlow Condensed` and `Source Sans 3` from Google Fonts as structural substitutes. They are not claimed as official GDIT fonts. The fallback stack preserves the condensed industrial headline character and high-legibility body rhythm.

## Visual grammar
- dark technical fields with bright linear accents;
- bold uppercase editorial headlines;
- angular lines, data paths and high-contrast information panels;
- imagery and language centered on mission technology, real-time operations, border security and decision advantage;
- purposeful movement through staged operational relationships.

## Role-derived translation
The original campaign metaphor is the **Cross-Domain Service Airlock**. It aligns the role's five cloud migration wave gates with five release conditions: mission value, data lineage, explainability, human authority and operational ownership. Motion represents a capability moving through the complete release path; scenario interaction changes release posture, evidence and decision ownership.

## Artifact application
- Website: full GDIT-derived color and typographic system, dark mission field, animated airlock.
- Resume and cover letter: restrained blue rules, condensed headings and single shared candidate header.
- Interview brief and 90-day plan: dark cover band, blue evidence tables and lime release-ready accent.

## Independent-candidate distinction
Every public surface identifies the work as an independent candidate vision and states that it is not an official GDIT or CBP publication and does not imply endorsement.
